Dear Anna

Advice for Everyday Life... Usually.

A lighthearted parody advice column.

Dear Anna,

I never thought I'd be writing one of these, but after months of careful observation, I believe I have enough evidence to ask for help.

My boss is a great guy. Fair, approachable, supportive, and genuinely cares about his employees. Unfortunately, he has developed what can only be described as a highly caffeinated condition.

Every morning begins the same way.

Before any discussion of sales goals, customer concerns, or priorities, we receive the Daily Coffee Briefing. We learn what blend was selected, whether it was brewed at home or purchased, how many ounces remain, and if today's cup is outperforming yesterday's.

Then come the updates.

"Second cup."

"This one really woke me up."

"I don't know about you, but I feel great."

Next call later...

"How about I order something for you?"

By noon, every rep knows more about this man's caffeine intake than the quarterly budget.

We've stopped telling time by the clock.

"It's about 10:30... should be 84 ounces in."

"I'll finish that project before his afternoon refill."

One rep claims she can identify the day of the week solely by the enthusiasm level of the coffee review. Another swears he can smell a slow drip from two counties away.

I've even caught myself asking, "How's the coffee today?" before saying good morning. I don't know when it happened, but somewhere along the way I became emotionally invested in a beverage I wasn't even drinking.

The strange part is... none of us actually mind.

The coffee reports have somehow become part of the team routine. If one morning passed without hearing about the roast, we'd probably assume Z-Scaler was down or someone had called in sick.

So my question is this:

Should we stage a caffeine intervention... or should we simply accept that every successful district needs one person willing to keep morale high, one coffee review at a time?

— Fully Brewed

Dear Fully Brewed,

Some managers inspire through speeches. Others motivate with spreadsheets. Yours appears to lead through espresso-based transparency.

As long as the work gets done, I'd consider the daily coffee commentary less of a workplace hazard and more of a recurring office sitcom. Every workplace has its quirks, and yours just happens to smell like fresh coffee.

If the biggest challenge your team faces is surviving another passionate discussion about roast profiles and refill number four, I'd say you're all doing just fine.

And to your boss:

I hope you read all the way to the end to realize this is a joke.
Thanks for being a good sport, Boss! ☕
